Q: Explain covalent bond in oxygen
A: In the case of oxygen (O₂), a **double covalent bond** is formed.

Here's why:
*   Each oxygen atom has 6 valence electrons. To achieve a stable octet (8 valence electrons), each oxygen atom needs 2 more electrons.
*   Instead of transferring electrons, the two oxygen atoms **share two pairs of electrons**. This means each oxygen atom contributes two electrons to the shared pool, forming two covalent bonds between them.
*   This sharing is represented as O=O.

As discussed earlier, a double covalent bond is stronger than a single covalent bond because more electrons are shared between the nuclei, leading to a stronger attraction and a shorter bond length. This is why O₂ is a stable molecule.

Q: Explain ionic bond in NaCl
A: An **ionic bond** is formed by the **transfer of electrons** between atoms. In the case of sodium chloride (NaCl):

1.  **Sodium (Na)** is a metal and has 1 valence electron. It readily **loses** this electron to achieve a stable electron configuration, forming a positive ion, Na$^+$.
2.  **Chlorine (Cl)** is a non-metal and has 7 valence electrons. It needs 1 more electron to complete its octet. It **gains** the electron lost by sodium, forming a negative ion, Cl$^-$.

This transfer of an electron from sodium to chlorine creates oppositely charged ions (Na$^+$ and Cl$^-$). The strong **electrostatic force of attraction** between these positive and negative ions is what forms the ionic bond in NaCl.

The process can be represented as:

$$ \text{NaCl} \rightarrow \text{Na}^+ + \text{Cl}^- $$

Ionic compounds like NaCl have a three-dimensional arrangement of these ions, held together by these strong electrostatic forces.

Q: Explain total valence electrons and bond pair electrons
A: The total valence electrons in a molecule are the sum of all valence electrons of the individual atoms that make up the molecule. These electrons are located in the outermost shell of an atom and are involved in forming chemical bonds.

Bond pair electrons are the electrons that are shared between two atoms to form a covalent bond. These electrons are typically found in pairs, with one electron contributed by each atom involved in the bond. The number of bond pair electrons in a molecule directly corresponds to the number of covalent bonds formed.
