Q: What are dibasic acid?
A: Dibasic acids are acids that can donate two protons (H⁺ ions) per molecule when they dissociate in water.

For example, sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄) is a dibasic acid because it can donate two protons in two separate steps:
1. H₂SO₄ → H⁺ + HSO₄⁻
2. HSO₄⁻ → H⁺ + SO₄²⁻

Another example is carbonic acid (H₂CO₃), which dissociates as follows:
1. H₂CO₃ → H⁺ + HCO₃⁻
2. HCO₃⁻ → H⁺ + CO₃²⁻
